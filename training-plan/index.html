Autoregulate — a difficult week of sleep, illness, or life stress is a valid reason to reduce Thursday quality to easy or cut Saturday mileage. The plan is a guide, not a contract.
Donut Run 10K Saturday, September 5, 2026 — tempo effort

Key principle: This race is your calibration tool. The result tells you what you're capable of right now, which removes guesswork from goal-setting for the rest of the season. Run it honestly — not a time trial, but a genuine effort that reflects your current fitness.

Lighthouse 5K Wednesday, June 3, 2026 — goal: tune-up, pace lock

Key principle: The 5K is a sustained hard effort from start to finish — there is no comfortable middle section. Use this race to lock in 5K pace and gather data for the Donut Run 10K and Big Bad Wolfe later in the season. Trust the work.

Columbus 10K Sunday, June 7, 2026 — goal: sub-11:00/mile

Key principle: This is an honest 10K early in the season. Your first race tells you what you're working with. You'll have completed 9 weeks of base and short interval work. Run it smart: negative splits are the goal. Going out too hard means you will feel it badly at mile 4 with nowhere to go.

Big Bad Wolfe 10 Miler Sunday, September 27, 2026 — paced effort

Key principle: This is not an all-out race — it's a strong, sustained effort with no taper. You've run 10 miles in training three times already; the distance is familiar. Run it hard enough to be meaningful, controlled enough to recover in time for the HM build. The data you gather here — average HR, how the effort felt at miles 5 and 8, how quickly you recover — directly informs your RTFTH pacing three weeks later.

Run to Feed the Hungry Half Saturday, November 14, 2026 — goal: A race

Key principle: This is what the whole season was built for. Your long run peaked at 12 miles, you've run a 10-mile race effort, and you arrive here with a genuine taper behind you. The Big Bad Wolfe told you what sustained hard effort feels like over distance — use that data to set your opening pace and run the second half faster than the first.
