Donut Run 5K Saturday, June 28, 2026 — Week 13

Key principle: This race is your calibration tool. The result tells you what you're capable of right now, which removes guesswork from goal-setting for the rest of the season. Run it honestly — not a time trial, but a genuine effort that reflects your current fitness.

Lighthouse 5K Wednesday, June 3, 2026 — goal: tune-up, pace lock

Key principle: The 5K is a sustained hard effort from start to finish — there is no comfortable middle section. Use this race to lock in 5K pace and gather data for Lemon Trails three weeks later. Trust the work.

Columbus 10K Sunday, June 7, 2026 — goal: sub-11:00/mile

Key principle: This is an honest 10K early in the season. Your first race tells you what you're working with. You'll have completed 9 weeks of base and short interval work. Run it smart: negative splits are the goal. Going out too hard means you will feel it badly at mile 4 with nowhere to go.

Lemon Trails 5K Saturday, July 18, 2026 — goal: sub-10:00/mile

Key principle: The 5K is a sustained hard effort from start to finish — there is no comfortable middle section. The Thursday interval sessions in weeks 12–16 have trained your body specifically for this. You arrive here tapered and rested off a lighter week. Trust the work.

Big Bad Wolfe 10 Miler Sunday, September 27, 2026 — paced effort

Key principle: You arrive at this race with a 12-mile long run and 24-mile week just completed. The fitness is there. The goal is intelligence, not a time. A controlled 10-mile effort three weeks before Columbus is the ideal dress rehearsal — run it smart and you'll know exactly what effort to target on October 18.

Columbus Half Marathon Sunday, October 18, 2026 — goal: fitness will determine

Key principle: You will arrive at Columbus with three long runs of 10-12 miles in the preceding four weeks, including the Big Bad Wolfe. That is an unusually strong preparation. The Big Bad Wolfe data removes the guesswork — you know what a sustained hard effort feels like over distance. Run the first half conservatively, run the second half with intent.

Run to Feed the Hungry Half Saturday, November 14, 2026 — goal: have fun

Key principle: This is the culmination of a 33-week season. You'll have run two half marathons' worth of race experience, a 10-mile paced effort, a 5K, and a 10K. Your long run has reached 12 miles three times. The fitness is the deepest it has ever been. Run with the confidence that comes from having done the work — and run the second half faster than the first.
